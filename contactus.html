Some Frequently Asked Questions(FAQ) about our department

Q1: Are there research opportunities for graduate students?

A: Yes, we offer research opportunities for graduate students in areas such as machine learning, robotics, and computer networks. Students can collaborate with faculty on cutting-edge research projects.

Q2: How can I get involved in extracurricular activities related to computer science?

A: The department sponsors various student organizations and clubs focused on computer science. Check our Events page for information on upcoming activities and how to get involved.

Q3: What resources are available in the computer labs?

A: Our computer labs are equipped with the latest hardware and software for programming, data analysis, and development. They include high-speed internet access, printers, and support staff for assistance.

Q4: How can I connect with faculty members for academic guidance?

A: We encourage students to attend office hours, schedule appointments, or participate in departmental events to interact with faculty members. They are readily available to provide academic support and guidance.

Q5: Are there internship opportunities for computer science students?

A: Absolutely! We have partnerships with industry leaders, and our students often secure internships to gain real-world experience. Visit our Career Services office for more information.

Q6: Can I pursue a minor in computer science if I'm majoring in another field?

A: Yes, we offer a minor in computer science for students majoring in other disciplines. It provides a foundational understanding of key computer science concepts.

Q7: Can I study abroad as a computer science major?

A: Absolutely! We have exchange programs and partnerships with universities around the world. Studying abroad can provide a global perspective and enrich your academic experience.

Q8: What programming languages are emphasized in the curriculum?

A: The curriculum covers a range of programming languages, including but not limited to Java, Python, C++, and JavaScript. The goal is to provide a well-rounded understanding of different programming paradigms.

Q9: How are students prepared for careers in the tech industry?

A: Our programs include career development initiatives, workshops, and connections with industry professionals. We also have a dedicated career services team to assist with job placement and internships.

Q10: Is there a computer science club or student organization?

A: Yes, we have an active computer science club that organizes events, hackathons, and networking opportunities. It's a great way to connect with peers who share similar interests.
